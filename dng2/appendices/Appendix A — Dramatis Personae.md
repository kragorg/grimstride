# Appendix A — Dramatis Personae

Adventurers
: The primary group consisting of
[Doctor Pepe](dng2--doctor-pepe.html),
[Elara Starglimmer](dng2--elara-starglimmer.html),
[Kragor Grimstride](dng2--kragor-grimstride.html),
[Scarlet Tanager](dng2--scaralet-tanager.html),
and [Therin Sunscale](dng2--therin-sunscale.html).
The former four
have been hailed as heroes in Palebank Village
for saving a local family from
a frozen fate due to the Aeorian-engineered disease, Frigid Woe;
and have the appreciation of Uthodurn
for delivering that plague into the kingdom’s safe-keeping.
Therin is their latest associate,
met during their joint assignment
to protect the transfer
of a powerful Aeorian artifact
to Uthodurn—a mission that ended
in a devastating ambush.

Ava Endlewood
: Proprietress of the Plexus Post, an antique and curiosity shop
on the Liber Disk of Uthodurn.
A woman of wide shoulders and fine, long grey-green hair,
she stocks rare magical items
and has hinted at a willingness
to hire the party for less-than-legitimate acquisitions.

Borric
: A fastidious dwarven assistant at Padillia’s shop on the Liber Disk.
He handles fittings and measurements
with an air of grave professionalism.

Brennik Coalvein
: A young Glassblade recruit
and the sole surviving guard of the Palebank caravan,
pulled from the wreckage of the lead wagon.
His mentor was Sergeant Dolgrim Ashward,
whose death in the ambush hit the young recruit hard.
He lives on the Volition Disk near the copper mines.

Caelynn Whisperwind
: A hardy teamster and the sole surviving crew member
of the Palebank caravan.
She managed the Flot goats
and escaped the ambush by slipping into the blinding rain.

Deilin
: A slender young elf who works the forge
at the Anvil of Smeltborne.
She is mute but hears and understands everything said around her.

The Crimson Rider
: A mysterious, expressionless warrior
whose face resembled dry, stretched parchment.
Riding a fearsome Moorbounder and wielding a cursed glaive,
they led the ambush against the caravan
and successfully stole the Aeorian artifact.

Ërethyn Galewing
: Minister of the Diarchy of Uthodurn.
An elven woman with silver hair pulled back tight
and ink-stained fingers,
she authorized the Palebank caravan
and debriefed the party on its catastrophic outcome.
She revealed the Kryn Dynasty’s likely involvement in the ambush,
noted the inconsistency of undead among Kryn forces,
and named the adventurers Glassblade Friends of the Court.
She asked them not to pursue the stolen artifact further.

Elro Aldataur
: The elf ranger appointed as leader of Palebank Village.
Elro recruited our party
to investigate the freezing sickness afflicting some villagers,
and then again to protect the transport
of a powerful Aeorian artifact to Uthodurn.

> The village is guided by Elro Aldataur, a weathered elf ranger who helped
> found Palebank roughly sixty years ago. Stoic and humorless, Elro showed a
> knack for leadership when the fledgling outpost was beset with danger on a
> near-constant basis. Elro earned the trust and respect of the residents, and
> they asked him to govern Palebank. He begrudgingly accepted, and has since
> overseen the expansion and protection of the village. Elro generally has
> sixty to ninety Glassblades at his command.
>
> -- _Explorer’s Guide to Wildemount_

Gelidon, the Nightmare in Ivory
: A fabled ancient white dragon
whose terrifying silhouette was spotted
soaring far to the south during the caravan's crossing
of the Crystalsands Tundra.
Faelan Duskwood warned
that if she caught their scent,
she would make a meal of the Flot goats.

Former Adventurers
: Whisper and Halite left the party in Syrinlya
to guide a lucrative expedition into Aeor.
Gerhard departed shortly after,
reuniting with his searching brother
and returning to Icehaven to rebuild his shattered life.

Hildra Bronzebuckle
: A teamster with the Palebank caravan.
Killed during the ambush in the Crystalsands Tundra.

Honor and Magic Liel-Tethwick
: Twin tiefling daughters of the Liel-Tethwick family.
Spared from the icy grip of the Frigid Woe,
they touchingly welcomed the adventurers back
to Palebank Village with woven floral garlands.

Luda
: Proprietress of the Broken Stool,
a tavern on the Volition Disk of Uthodurn.
An older female dwarf with grey-brown hair,
mutton-chop sideburns, and old scars on her chin and clavicle.
She provided the party lodging and meals on their arrival
and proved a useful source of local gossip and shopping recommendations.

Kord, The Stormlord
: God of battle, roaring tempests, and unyielding strength.
Kragor’s mother worshipped him in absolute secrecy.
Kragor recently experienced a profound vision of her
and received a lingering, warming blessing
at an ancient mountain shrine dedicated to the god.

Padillia
: Proprietress of Padillia’s Boutique of Ample Armaments
and Boutique of Bountiful Provisions on the Liber Disk.
A tall, broad-shouldered Attalwen with copper-colored hair.
Her assistant Borric handles fittings and measurements.

Mireth Copperleaf
: Captain of the Palebank caravan escort.
A stoic attalwen (an elf with a thin beard).
Killed during the devastating caravan ambush
in the Crystalsands Tundra.

Dolgrim Ashward
: Sergeant of the Palebank caravan escort
and mentor to the young recruit Brennik Coalvein.
A grizzled dwarven veteran.
Killed during the caravan ambush
in the Crystalsands Tundra.

Faelan Duskwood
: A veteran Glassblade killed during the caravan ambush.
He identified the distant winged silhouette
as possibly being Gelidon, the Nightmare in Ivory.

Oryn Hammerglass
: A veteran Glassblade killed during the caravan ambush.

Talindra Snowveil
: A veteran Glassblade killed during the caravan ambush.

Quajath
: An ancient and powerful entity,
referred to as “the Worm”,
that slumbers deep beneath the ice of Eiselcross.
Its dreams are a psychic contagion,
infecting the minds of the lost and promising warmth and communion,
but delivering only madness and servitude.
It targeted Kragor with its telepathic dreams,
causing him immense pain
and relentlessly hunting him through its followers,
the Wormkin.
The party recently learned these fanatic wildlings
are locally known as “Mawcotters”,
an obsessive cult that undertakes dark pilgrimages
to harvest and consume the frozen worm’s flesh.

Reani
: An aasimar who lives near the Anvil of Smeltborne
on the Liber Disk of Uthodurn.
She is responsible for the profusion of flowers
decorating Umagorn's forge.
Described by Umagorn as having a heart of gold
but very serious in temperament.

Rimeflake
: A white dragon hatched
from an egg discovered by the party
on the Foren plains.
Kragor carried the egg
back to the Greying Wildlands,
watched over it until its hatching,
and fed it for a few days
until it was grown enough
to fly into the mountains
to do its own hunting.
“Rimeflake” and just “Flake” are Kragor’s nicknames
for the wyrmling.

Sparky
: Scarlet’s owl wild companion. A loyal and effective scout, Sparky provides aerial reconnaissance for the party.

Tomás Veer
: A teamster with the Palebank caravan.
Killed instantly when the purple worm
destroyed the lead wagon.

Tormund Ironfell
: Commander of the Uthodurn gate garrison.
A broad dwarf of late middle age
with an iron-grey beard trimmed short and deep-set eyes.
He received the battered caravan survivors at the gate
and sent the party to the Broken Stool
while he consulted with the palace.

Umagorn Smeltborne
: Master smith at the Anvil of Smeltborne on the Liber Disk.
A young dwarf with long blonde braided hair
and a magnificent braided mustache.
His forge is surrounded by flowers,
courtesy of a local aasimar named Reani.
He accepted commissions from the party
for enchanted weapons and elven chain.

Thilda
: A perpetually exhausted dwarven pelt-sharer
in Palebank Village,
who became the primary beneficiary
of Doctor Pepe’s lavish spending and hedonism.

Waffles
: Kragor’s bat familiar.
