Session Summaries

Interlude

Following their hard-won victory against the Frigid Woe, the adventurers return to Syrinlya to celebrate their newfound fortune. However, the road ahead beckons the companions in different directions. Whisper and Halite accept a lucrative contract to guide a new expedition into Aeor, while Gerhard is reunited with his brother and decides to return home to rebuild his life. The remaining party members—Doctor Pepe, Elara, Kragor, and Scarlet—set sail back to Palebank Village.

Upon their arrival, they receive a heroes’ welcome from the saved townspeople and a formal letter of introduction to the Uthodurnian court. Amidst the joyous celebrations and Elara’s captivating performances at the Jolly Dwarf, a quiet secret looms. Kragor hides the salvaged white dragon egg in a secluded, frozen ravine, checking on it weekly. Soon, the egg hatches into a ravenous wyrmling he names Rimeflake. Kragor nurtures and guards the beast in secret until its wings are strong enough to carry it into the frosted hills. With the dragon gone and their business in Palebank concluded, the remaining heroes prepare to look toward their next great destination.

The Road to Uthodurn

As the adventurers plan their next move with their newfound wealth, the village leader Elro Aldataur offers them a high-stakes contract: escorting a caravan carrying a secretive, powerful Aeorian artifact overland to the mountainside city of Uthodurn. Joined by a squad of elite Glassblades and a devout dragonborn cleric named Therin Sunscale, the party embarks on a grueling journey across the frozen Crystalsands Tundra.

The tranquility of the march is shattered on the seventh night when a devastating ambush strikes the camp. A colossal purple worm bursts from the frozen earth, flanked by shadow-draped ghasts and a silent, parchment-faced warrior riding a deadly Moorbounder. The assault is swift and merciless; several guards and teamsters are slain, and Therin is nearly felled. Despite the heroes’ desperate defense, the mysterious rider escapes with the primary artifact.

Left with only a smaller chest of relics, a traumatized young recruit named Brennik, and one surviving teamster, the battered companions must press on. Along the treacherous mountain trails, Kragor finds a hidden shrine to Kord, receiving a warm divine blessing after offering his mother’s heirloom. With the druid Scarlet twice wildshaping into a massive Flot goat to haul the overloaded wagons up the steepest slopes, the weary survivors push through the freezing night to finally reach the majestic gates of Uthodurn, carrying grim tidings for the Diarchy.

Uthodurn

Having survived the devastating ambush on the Crystalsands Tundra, the battered companions finally gain entry into the grand underground city of Uthodurn. Taken in under a strict order of silence by the stern Commander Tormund Ironfell, they seek refuge at the Broken Stool tavern. As they rest and recover, they hear whispers of escalating tensions between the Kryn Dynasty and the Dwendalian Empire, and Elara learns of another celestial being, Reani, living within the city.

The following morning, the adventurers explore the bustling Deliberation Disk, preparing themselves for the challenges ahead. At the Anvil of Smeltborne, Kragor trades an unusual Aeorian sword to commission custom-enchanted armaments, while Doctor Pepe and Scarlet spend their fortunes at Padillia’s emporium on powerful magic items and sturdy armor. Later, at the Plexus Post, the group stocks up on essential arcane and divine components, including a holy symbol of Bahamut for their new companion, Therin.

The centerpiece of their day is a tense audience with Minister Ërethyn Galewing. After delivering the surviving Aeorian relics and detailing the ambush, the Minister points out a troubling contradiction: the attackers utilized beasts of the Kryn Dynasty alongside necromantic undead, forces that are traditionally bitter enemies. Though paid in full and granted the prestigious ‘Friends of the Court’ tokens, the heroes are forbidden from investigating the stolen artifact further. Respectful of the mandate but deeply curious, the party decides to check on the traumatized young recruit Brennik before deciding their next move.

The Luddenbrock Mines

Returning from their audience with the Minister, the adventurers are overjoyed to reunite with their long-lost companions, Halite and Whisper, who have returned from Eiselcross. After finishing their business at Padillia’s emporium—where Elara secures a magically dramatic billowing cloak—and enjoying some local black moss cupcakes, the expanded party heads to the Volition Disk to check on the young recruit Brennik.

At the Coalvein home, a tense debate over the merits of service is interrupted by news of an ancient danger. Brennik’s father, Torvin, describes a mysterious incident deep within the Luddenbrock mines, where a crew broke through a fissure, exposing a sickly green glow and a terrifying presence that left a miner catatonic.

Venturing into the sealed depths, the heroes breach the wall and discover a long-forgotten magic chamber containing a binding circle and pulsing crystal pillars. There, they are brutally ambushed by a grotesque, wolf-like daemon with psychic teeth, alongside a ruby golem. During a desperate, chaotic struggle, Kragor barely survives a reckless gambit to freeze the beast, allowing Doctor Pepe to deliver a flawless, fatal blow.

In the aftermath, the companions explore an adjacent hidden study where a desiccated corpse, Kaspien, sits in eternal vigil over a cracked crystal vial containing a trapped, unmoving spark of light. Guided by Kaspien’s frantic bat-homunculus and wielding a commanding ruby amulet, the party uncovers letters revealing a tragic experiment in magical immortality. Kaspien and his colleague Margrethe had sought eternal life, but a critical design flaw fractured the soul vessel upon transfer. Realizing the homunculus’s continued survival means Kaspien’s soul remains trapped in agonizing stasis within the cracked vial, the heroes are left with a profound moral dilemma.

Dust and Diplomacy

In the quiet of Kaspien’s hidden study, the adventurers interrogate the wizard’s bat-homunculus, Flea, who reveals his master’s soul is trapped within the fragile, cracked crystal vial. However, a well-intentioned attempt by Kragor to retrieve the vial with a spectral hand goes disastrously wrong. The vial shatters, Kaspien crumbles to dust, and Flea pops out of existence.

Before the arriving Glassblades, led by Commander Hilda Brackmar, can secure the ruins, the party spins a clever half-truth to cover their tracks. Kragor smuggles out a forbidden book on wizardly lichdom while Scarlet keeps the ruby golem’s control amulet secret. Later, at the Vellum Steeple archives, Kragor strategically surrenders the book to earn the favor of Scribewarden Ressia Uvesic, securing a valuable research discount to investigate the obscure Mawcotter cult.

The political stakes skyrocket when the heroes are summoned to the royal palace for a tense audience with Minister Galewing, Commander Ironfell, and the Taskhand—an ambassador from the rival Kryn Dynasty. Accused of orchestrating the caravan ambush, the defensive drow ambassador is vindicated when the party reveals the presence of necromantic shadowghasts, a force the Kryn vehemently despise. The Taskhand reveals that a squad of Kryn scouts was recently slaughtered and their purple worm stolen. Recognizing a mutual enemy, the Diarchy and the Dynasty strike a fragile truce, offering the adventurers a lucrative contract, mount provisions, and safe passage to investigate the slaughter site of the Kryn scouts in the treacherous southern wilds.

Preparations and Purple Worms

Preparing to venture south, the companions gather at the Broken Stool to recover and coordinate. Their planning is briefly interrupted by a raucous bar brawl, quickly resolved by the formidable innkeeper Luda, followed by a dramatic visit from the Kryn Ambassador, Taskhand Serath Mirimm. Flanked by elite Echo Knights, the ambassador invites the party to a summit at the Kryn Embassy. A seasoned caravan runner, Donal, warns the party to avoid the corrupted Savalirwood—rumored to harbor a monstrous, four-armed demon called an udaak—recommending instead a swift crossing of the Rime Plains.

The next morning, the heroes divide to tackle final errands. While Scarlet receives a divine blessing and an herbalism kit at a hidden grotto-shrine to the Wildmother, Doctor Pepe coordinates a daring heist inside the Vellum Steeple. Using Elara’s invisibility magic and Kragor’s distraction regarding the grotesque lore of the flesh-eating Mawcotter cult, Doctor Pepe successfully steals an ancient leather map case containing a valuable, masterfully crafted map of Tal’Dorei’s Cliffkeep Mountains.

At the Kryn Embassy, over a meal of frostworm stew, the party negotiates their expedition route with Taskhand Serath Mirimm. They map out a hybrid travel plan utilizing Uthodurn’s Flot goats for mountain passes and horses for a high-speed dash across the open Rime Plains, secured with a Kryn medallion of safe passage. After Kragor subtly enchants a stubborn Torvin Coalvein to permit his son Brennik to join the escort, the party spends their final night relaxing. Elara delivers a spellbinding performance at the high-class Underrest club, where Minister Galewing personally emphasizes the crucial weight of their peace-keeping mission before the heroes depart the subterranean safety of Uthodurn.

The Bigger They Are, The Harder They Grovel

The morning of their departure from Uthodurn begins with a final luxurious breakfast and a scramble for gear. Led by the no-nonsense Glassblade commander Hilda Brackmar, the companions settle on horses rather than sure-footed goats, preparing for a rapid, high-stakes crossing of the Rime Plains. Joined by Kryn Taskhand Serath Mirimm and his heavily armored Echo Knights, the expedition ventures into the steep mountain passes. Along the way, the party avoids a hungry wyvern, bypasses a herd of white bison, and spots the ancient white dragon Gelidon soaring ominously overhead.

The relative peace is shattered when the group is caught in a devastating pincer ambush by two towering frost giants. Boulders rain down, and a fifteen-foot giant savages Kragor with a pine-sized arrow. Scarlet wildshapes into a giant wolf spider to scout the approaching giantess while Echo Knights Aedric and Vornesh manifest their spectral duplicates to defend the Taskhand. Young recruit Brennik overcomes his terror, joining the fray to support Kragor.

The battle spirals into chaos as the first giant downs Elara with a brutal greataxe blow, sending a wave of panic through the companions. Promptly revived by the Taskhand’s timely healing magic, Elara recovers her composure and turns the tide of the battle. While Hilda ruthlessly executes the first giant, Elara uses an authoritative command to force the rampaging giantess to grovel in the snow. Completely swarmed by the coordinated might of the adventurers, Glassblades, and drow knights, the giantess is swiftly brought down, leaving the battered survivors to catch their breath amidst the freezing mountain silence.

Runaways and Rockslides

Following their hard-won victory against the frost giants, the battered companions must immediately deal with the chaotic aftermath. Several terrified horses have bolted into the crags, forcing a coordinated retrieval effort. While Doctor Pepe and a wildshaped Scarlet successfully recover the runaways, a reprimand from Taskhand Serath Mirimm to his guard Aedric hints at internal tensions. The group seeks refuge in a hidden Kryn military cache concealed behind an illusory stone wall, where Doctor Pepe notices a suspicious false back wall.

The journey turns disastrous the following day when a massive avalanche cascades down a narrow gorge, burying the dragonborn cleric Therin and the drow guard Vornesh. In a desperate, high-stakes rescue, Therin uses his fire breath to create a vent, and Vornesh swaps places with her spectral echo, while a giant-sized Hilda and a wildshaped Scarlet-bear dig them out of the snow. With the mountain pass permanently choked and time running out, the Taskhand reveals a closely guarded secret: an underground passage through the Underdark that bypasses the mountains entirely.

Leaving the horses behind with Commander Hilda Brackmar, who returns to Uthodurn, young recruit Brennik remains as the city’s sole representative. The party backtracks to the Kryn cache and steps through the false back wall, revealing a perfectly smooth, worm-like tunnel that plunges deep into the earth. Stepping into the vast, natural cavern complexes below, the companions leave the surface world behind to brave the terrifying, legendary wonders of the sunless depths.

The Underdark

Swallowed by the stone, the companions embark on a grueling descent into the patient, ancient depths of the Underdark. Led by Taskhand Serath Mirimm, who conjures dim, violet-tinted lights to avoid attracting subterranean predators, the party navigates the eerie silence. Along the way, they scavenge edible fungi, drink from gelatinous water orbs, and loot the skeletal remains of an ancient duergar-drow skirmish. But the sunless miles quickly take a punishing physical and mental toll on the surface dwellers.

The trek’s first major obstacle arrives at a freezing, roaring underground river. Scarlet wildshapes into a giant octopus to bridge the gap with a rope, while the drow Echo Knights utilize their spectral teleportation to secure the line on the far bank. The crossing is treacherous: Brennik nearly drowns, and both Doctor Pepe and Therin are swept downstream before being rescued by Therin’s quick magic and Scarlet’s massive tentacles.

Exhausted but resolute, the party pushes into a glowing cavern of Faerzress energy, only to be brutally ambushed by three stony ropers. The camouflaged predators rain down tentacles, snaring and biting Vornesh and dragging Aedric and the Taskhand toward the ceiling. A frantic battle erupts. Elara barrages the beasts with her newly acquired rod of magic missiles, and Scarlet-moorbounder leaps impossibly high to claw the ceiling-clinging monsters. Leveraging his telekinetic will, Kragor wrenches his allies from the ropers’ clutches, dropping them to safety. Through a synchronized onslaught of spell, steel, and spectral echoes, the companions dismantle the ancient predators, proving their mettle in the unforgiving deep.

Sporeling’s Homecoming

In the immediate aftermath of the roper battle, the adventurers harvest a small fortune in gems and magic potions from the monsters’ remains. Exploring the cavern, they discover a desiccated humanoid corpse covered in pale filament. When Doctor Pepe accidentally triggers a defensive cloud of spore-dust, the group experiences a vivid, shared telepathic vision of fear, which leads them to a tiny, round myconid they affectionately name Sprout.

Desperate to return to their colony, Sprout uses their mind-linking spores to share a warm vision of their fungal home, coaxing the party to follow. Along the way, Sprout has Kragor lift them up to touch a vein of pink coral-fungus, temporarily connecting the companions’ minds to the collective consciousness of the entire myconid colony. Amidst a rush of alien emotions, they perceive a terrifying, violent conflict with strange, iron-spiked humanoids. Though the Kryn Taskhand Serath Mirimm urges them to stay on-mission at a subterranean crossroads, Kragor and the others advocate for a detour to guarantee Sprout’s safe homecoming.

Their arrival at the colony is met with immense gratitude. Escorted by myconids and towering, reanimated giant corpse guardians, they are ushered into a massive cavern to meet the Sovereign. After the Sovereign grants them a hospitable suite of rooms and restorative spore-blessings, Serath reveals the identity of the iron-spiked threat from their visions: the derro. Described as criminally insane, dangerous subterranean zealots driven by aberrant forces, the Kryn execute them on sight. With Therin predicting the Sovereign will request their aid against these mad Underdark dwellers, the companions rest, preparing for the diplomatic and moral challenges of the morning.
