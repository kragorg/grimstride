Appendix A — Dramatis Personae

Adventurers
The primary group consisting of Doctor Pepe, Elara Starglimmer, Halite the Goliath, Kragor Grimstride, Scarlet Tanager, Therin Sunscale, and Whisper of Misty Valley. Doctor Pepe, Elara, Kragor, and Scarlet have been hailed as heroes in Palebank Village for saving a local family from a frozen fate due to the Aeorian-engineered disease, Frigid Woe; and have the appreciation of Uthodurn for delivering that plague into the kingdom’s safe-keeping. Therin is their latest associate, met during their joint assignment to protect the transfer of a powerful Aeorian artifact to Uthodurn—a mission that ended in a devastating ambush. Halite and Whisper recently rejoined the group in Uthodurn following a guiding contract cut short by their client’s death in Eiselcross.
Ava Endlewood
Proprietress of the Plexus Post, an antique and curiosity shop on the Liber Disk of Uthodurn. A woman of wide shoulders and fine, long grey-green hair, she stocks rare magical items and has hinted at a willingness to hire the party for less-than-legitimate acquisitions.
Borric
A fastidious dwarven assistant at Padillia’s shop on the Liber Disk. He handles fittings and measurements with an air of grave professionalism.
Brennik Coalvein
A young Glassblade recruit and the sole surviving guard of the Palebank caravan, pulled from the wreckage of the lead wagon. His mentor was Sergeant Dolgrim Ashward, whose death in the ambush hit the young recruit hard. He lives on the Volition Disk near the copper mines.
Caelynn Whisperwind
A hardy teamster and the sole surviving crew member of the Palebank caravan. She managed the Flot goats and escaped the ambush by slipping into the blinding rain.
Cara
A worker at the Luddenbrock mines on the Volition Disk of Uthodurn. She peered through a fissure the mining crew had broken into and reported seeing something made of teeth before collapsing unconscious. The healers revived her, but she did not return to work.
The Crimson Rider
A mysterious, expressionless warrior whose face resembled dry, stretched parchment. Riding a fearsome Moorbounder and wielding a cursed glaive, they led the ambush against the caravan and successfully stole the Aeorian artifact.
Deilin
A slender young elf who works the forge at the Anvil of Smeltborne. She is mute but hears and understands everything said around her.
Dolgrim Ashward
Sergeant of the Palebank caravan escort and mentor to the young recruit Brennik Coalvein. A grizzled dwarven veteran. Killed during the caravan ambush in the Crystalsands Tundra.
Elro Aldataur
The elf ranger appointed as leader of Palebank Village. Elro recruited our party to investigate the freezing sickness afflicting some villagers, and then again to protect the transport of a powerful Aeorian artifact to Uthodurn.

The village is guided by Elro Aldataur, a weathered elf ranger who helped found Palebank roughly sixty years ago. Stoic and humorless, Elro showed a knack for leadership when the fledgling outpost was beset with danger on a near-constant basis. Elro earned the trust and respect of the residents, and they asked him to govern Palebank. He begrudgingly accepted, and has since overseen the expansion and protection of the village. Elro generally has sixty to ninety Glassblades at his command.

– Explorer’s Guide to Wildemount

Ërethyn Galewing
Minister of the Diarchy of Uthodurn. An elven woman with silver hair pulled back tight and ink-stained fingers, she authorized the Palebank caravan and debriefed the party on its catastrophic outcome. She revealed the Kryn Dynasty’s likely involvement in the ambush, noted the inconsistency of undead among Kryn forces, and named the adventurers Glassblade Friends of the Court. She asked them not to pursue the stolen artifact further.
Faelan Duskwood
A veteran Glassblade killed during the caravan ambush. He identified the distant winged silhouette as possibly being Gelidon, the Nightmare in Ivory.
Former Adventurers
Gerhard departed in Syrinlya, reuniting with his searching brother and returning to Icehaven to rebuild his shattered life.
Gelidon, the Nightmare in Ivory
A fabled ancient white dragon whose terrifying silhouette was spotted soaring far to the south during the caravan’s crossing of the Crystalsands Tundra. Faelan Duskwood warned that if she caught their scent, she would make a meal of the Flot goats.
Halite the Goliath
A Goliath and former member of the party who departed in Syrinlya alongside Whisper to guide a wealthy collector into Eiselcross. The client was killed by a remorhaz before the expedition had properly begun; they brought the body home and earned their fee. Halite and Whisper rejoined the party on the Auger Trail in Uthodurn.
Hilda
Proprietress of the Softer Stone Forge, a bakery on the Liber Disk two blocks from Padillia’s Boutiques. A stout female dwarf with grey hair pulled into a tight bun and wide, capable hands. Her black moss cupcake— earthy and mineral on the outside, matcha cream within— provoked contemplative silence from several members of the party.
Hildra Bronzebuckle
A teamster with the Palebank caravan. Killed during the ambush in the Crystalsands Tundra.
The Homunculus
A bat-like creature roughly the size of a large cat, bound to the wizard Kaspien. It speaks only Abyssal but understands Common perfectly well. Sustained by some dark arithmetic long past its master’s apparent death, it guarded the sealed laboratory beneath the Luddenbrock mines and endured the intrusion of the party with considerable outrage. Once the daemon was dispatched, it proved willing enough to answer questions— waiting, it seemed, for someone to ask the right one.
Honor and Magic Liel-Tethwick
Twin tiefling daughters of the Liel-Tethwick family. Spared from the icy grip of the Frigid Woe, they touchingly welcomed the adventurers back to Palebank Village with woven floral garlands.
Kaspien
A wizard whose desiccated remains occupy the writing desk of a sealed subterranean laboratory beneath the Luddenbrock mines. Long ago, Kaspien undertook an illicit soul-transfer ritual developed in secret collaboration with a colleague named Margrethe, concealing their work from an oversight body called the Forum. A letter from Margrethe containing critical last-minute corrections arrived unread: the ritual cracked the crystal vial meant to contain the soul, leaving Kaspien suspended in agonizing stasis— dead in body, not yet free in spirit.
Kord, The Stormlord
God of battle, roaring tempests, and unyielding strength. Kragor’s mother worshipped him in absolute secrecy. Kragor recently experienced a profound vision of her and received a lingering, warming blessing at an ancient mountain shrine dedicated to the god.
Luda
Proprietress of the Broken Stool, a tavern on the Volition Disk of Uthodurn. An older female dwarf with grey-brown hair, mutton-chop sideburns, and old scars on her chin and clavicle. She provided the party lodging and meals on their arrival and proved a useful source of local gossip and shopping recommendations.
Margrethe
A scholar based in Zemniaz and Kaspien’s collaborator in developing a forbidden process for magical immortality. Their work was hidden from the Forum, an institutional oversight body. Margrethe survived their own transition and sent Kaspien the adjustments needed to prevent a catastrophic resonance fracture— but the warning arrived unread.
Marva Coalvein
Brennik’s mother. An older dwarven woman who received the party at the Coalvein home on the Volition Disk with the wariness of someone who has had too many difficult conversations recently.
Mireth Copperleaf
Captain of the Palebank caravan escort. A stoic attalwen (an elf with a thin beard). Killed during the devastating caravan ambush in the Crystalsands Tundra.
Oryn Hammerglass
A veteran Glassblade killed during the caravan ambush.
Padillia
Proprietress of Padillia’s Boutique of Ample Armaments and Boutique of Bountiful Provisions on the Liber Disk. A tall, broad-shouldered Attalwen with copper-colored hair. Her assistant Borric handles fittings and measurements.
Quajath
An ancient and powerful entity, referred to as “the Worm”, that slumbers deep beneath the ice of Eiselcross. Its dreams are a psychic contagion, infecting the minds of the lost and promising warmth and communion, but delivering only madness and servitude. It targeted Kragor with its telepathic dreams, causing him immense pain and relentlessly hunting him through its followers, the Wormkin. The party recently learned these fanatic wildlings are locally known as “Mawcotters”, an obsessive cult that undertakes dark pilgrimages to harvest and consume the frozen worm’s flesh.
Reani
An aasimar who lives near the Anvil of Smeltborne on the Liber Disk of Uthodurn. She is responsible for the profusion of flowers decorating Umagorn’s forge. Described by Umagorn as having a heart of gold but very serious in temperament.
Rimeflake
A white dragon hatched from an egg discovered by the party on the Foren plains. Kragor carried the egg back to the Greying Wildlands, watched over it until its hatching, and fed it for a few days until it was grown enough to fly into the mountains to do its own hunting. “Rimeflake” and just “Flake” are Kragor’s nicknames for the wyrmling.
The Ruby Golem
A seven-foot humanoid construct of ruby-red crystal, encountered in the sealed chamber beneath the Luddenbrock mines. Built to guard the laboratory, it is bound to an amulet taken from Kaspien’s throat. After Scarlet commandeered the amulet, the golem now answers to her commands.
Sparky
Scarlet’s owl wild companion. A loyal and effective scout, Sparky provides aerial reconnaissance for the party.
Talindra Snowveil
A veteran Glassblade killed during the caravan ambush.
Thilda
A perpetually exhausted dwarven pelt-sharer in Palebank Village, who became the primary beneficiary of Doctor Pepe’s lavish spending and hedonism.
Tomás Veer
A teamster with the Palebank caravan. Killed instantly when the purple worm destroyed the lead wagon.
Tormund Ironfell
Commander of the Uthodurn gate garrison. A broad dwarf of late middle age with an iron-grey beard trimmed short and deep-set eyes. He received the battered caravan survivors at the gate and sent the party to the Broken Stool while he consulted with the palace.
Torvin Coalvein
Brennik’s father and a miner at the Luddenbrock mines. He holds that mining is honest work and made his view plain that the Glassblades are a dangerous romantic notion— a position rooted, as the party came to understand, in something older than simple preference. A portrait in the Coalvein home showed him and Dolgrim Ashward as young men, bright-eyed with the confidence of those who have not yet encountered the things that will cost them. It was Torvin who wanted the green glow in the mines investigated so work could resume.
Umagorn Smeltborne
Master smith at the Anvil of Smeltborne on the Liber Disk. A young dwarf with long blonde braided hair and a magnificent braided mustache. His forge is surrounded by flowers, courtesy of a local aasimar named Reani. He accepted commissions from the party for enchanted weapons and elven chain.
Waffles
Kragor’s bat familiar.
Whisper of Misty Valley
A former member of the party who departed in Syrinlya alongside Halite to guide a wealthy collector into Eiselcross. The client was killed by a remorhaz before the expedition had properly begun; they brought the body home and earned their fee. Whisper moves through crowded spaces with the unhurried ease of someone accustomed to being the most dangerous person in any crowd they care to enter. They rejoined the party on the Auger Trail in Uthodurn.
